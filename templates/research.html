5. Discussion

The results demonstrate that cross-asset OFI contains statistically significant, but economically modest predictive content for component stock returns. The Ridge model consistently outperforms both OLS and XGBoost, suggesting that the relationship between OFI and returns is primarily linear — consistent with Kyle (1985) and the market microstructure literature on linear price impact.

The signal half-life analysis reveals that OFI alpha decays within 5–15 minutes, indicating that this is a high-frequency signal. The regime analysis shows stronger performance in high-volatility environments, where order flow imbalance is more informative and mean-reversion patterns are more pronounced.

Critically, I tested whether complexity adds value over linear models — in most cases it did not, which actually strengthens the finding: it means the signal is robust and not an artifact of overfitting.
